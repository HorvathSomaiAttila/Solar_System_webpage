The Sun

The Sun is the massive star at the center of our solar system, providing light and energy to all the planets.

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet and the closest to the Sun, known for its scorching days and freezing nights.

Venus

Venus, often called Earth’s twin, has a thick, toxic atmosphere and surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead.

Earth

Earth is the only planet known to support life, with diverse ecosystems and abundant water.

Mars

Mars, the Red Planet, is home to the tallest volcano and some of the most fascinating exploration targets in the solar system.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet, famous for its giant red storm and over 70 moons.

Saturn

Saturn stands out with its stunning ring system made of ice and rock particles.

Uranus

Uranus is an icy giant that rotates on its side, giving it unique seasons and a bluish-green hue.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet, known for its deep blue color and the fastest winds in the solar system.
