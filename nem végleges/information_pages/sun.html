The Sun

About the Sun

The Sun is the Solar System's star and by far its most massive component. Its large mass which comprises 99.86% of all the mass in the Solar System, produces temperatures and densities in its core high enough to sustain nuclear fusion of hydrogen into helium. The Sun is a population I star, having formed in the spiral arms of the Milky Way galaxy.

  • Diameter: 1,391,000 km
  • Orbit: 0 AU from the Sun
  • Interesting Fact: The Sun's mass makes up about 99.86% of the entire mass of the Solar System. This means the Sun "dominates" the gravitational influence of the Solar System.
