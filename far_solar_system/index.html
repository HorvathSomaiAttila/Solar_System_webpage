Pluto, the dwarf planet, lies in the icy Kuiper Belt and is known for its heart-shaped glacier and mysterious surface.

The most famous periodic comet, Halley's Comet, dazzles Earth every 76 years with its radiant tail and storied past.

Haumea

A dwarf planet with an unusual elongated shape, Haumea stands out with its rapid rotation and icy surface.

A dwarf planet more massive than Pluto, Eris resides in the distant Kuiper Belt, challenging our definition of planets.
